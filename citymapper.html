Hi, Citymapper!

Here's my take on a cool new feature.

Something that is very painful in city mobility is to feel like a victim of a system you have no choice but bow to and use everyday. When commuting, people get easily used to routinely being in overcrowded train and buses, since they are in a hurry to get home, to "escape" the urban space.

Giving users options beyond transport

What if, instead of showing people the fastest way to get home and be safe locked out of the chaos of the city, we gave them suggestions on how to enjoy the places near where they are, while waiting for traffic to calm down, and get home more comfortably a little bit later?

Citymapper could use integrations with Foursquare, Yelp, Wundelist, Facebook, Swarm, Calendar to predict things the user might want to do and use them to make suggestions.

This could also mean telling users to go earlier next time:

“Hey, this route is usually less crowded before 7pm. Hurry a bit tomorrow and spend 25 less minutes getting home.”

Or notify them when is a goot time to get going:

“Hey, now is a good time to go! Leave work by 6h30 and save 25 minutes”

Anyway, these are just quick, silly ideas I had. I'd be honored to be part of the team and help you guys build truly transformative city apps.
