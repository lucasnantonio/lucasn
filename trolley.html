Retrolley

Airline waste is one of those complex problems that have many variables, many stakeholders, and as of now, no one is taking responsibility for it. As designers, we had the opportunity to look closely and broadly at this context, and identify key issues that could lead us to a possible solution. We visited Embraer, a brazilian aircraft producer, visited airports and flew a couple of times to observe and talk with passengers and crew.

Our proposal: redesigning the trolley

During a flight, passenger’s waste is put away in a hurry so the plane can land quickly, and it is currently done haphazardly. Everything is basically thrown in the same bag, without any separation and taking lots of space. The consequences is that the output, when the plane lands, is mostly unworkable and really hard to recycle. If we only could separate the materials, keep them dry, and make them use less space would be a great improvement.

Stacking, sorting, and compressing

The product created is a viable solution for this problems, optimizing both in flight and ground procedures. With it, it is possible to sort organic and recyclable garbage, pile up plastic cups, thus using 30 times less space; liquids have a specific container, and the garbage can be manually compacted for 30% space reduction.

Designed as a student for the University of São Paulo

Team: Lucas Neumann, Liana Maki, Lucas Otsuka, Denise Ikuno, Tadeu Omae
